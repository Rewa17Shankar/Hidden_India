This place came to light when in the 8th century, Adiguru Shri Shankaracharya established a Matha (Jyotir Math or Shri Shankaracharya Math) at Joshimath, about 6.6 km from Auli, which still exists today. After the establishment of the monastery, this place also came to be known as the "Gateway of the Himalayas". After the establishment of this math, Adi Shankaracharya Ji moved forward like Badrinath. Semi-nomadic tribes of Tibetans, Mongolians and Bhotias conducted trade and communication between Tibet (China) and India from here. They took the help of their long haired yaks and horses to cross the snowy mountains of the Himalayas. The popular Trishul peak (23,490 feet) is the scene of an unusual expedition that took place here in 1958. It took the mountaineers almost four days to climb and about 90 minutes to ski down to the base.


Auli and Joshimath are situated in the high Himalayas bordering China. In the Sino-Indian War of 1962, there have been several initiatives by Chinese troops to cross the borders and enter Indian territory. During the conflict, the residents of the border village supported the Indian Army. Auli ropeway was constructed in September 1993 to enhance tourism in Auli. At an altitude of about 10,000 feet, this ropeway serves as a transport between Joshimath and Auli.

Majestic Valley: The Heart of Auli's Natural Splendor

Majestic Valley in Auli is a captivating destination that truly lives up to its name. Surrounded by towering Himalayan peaks and lush, verdant landscapes, this valley is a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike. The sweeping vistas of snow-capped mountains, dense forests, and sprawling meadows create a picturesque setting that feels almost otherworldly.

Visitors to Majestic Valley are often struck by the serene atmosphere, which offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. The valley is also a popular spot for trekking and skiing, especially during the winter months when the entire area is blanketed in snow. In the summer, the valley transforms into a vibrant tapestry of wildflowers, making it an ideal destination for those looking to explore the natural beauty of Auli.

The clear skies and unpolluted environment of Majestic Valley also make it a perfect spot for stargazing, offering a breathtaking view of the night sky. Whether you're seeking adventure or tranquility, Majestic Valley in Auli provides an unforgettable experience that showcases the best of the Himalayan region. .

Trishul Peak: A Towering Giant of Auli

Trishul Peak, one of the most prominent mountains in the Garhwal region, stands tall at an elevation of 7,120 meters, making it a majestic sight in Auli. Named after the trident of Lord Shiva, Trishul Peak is actually a group of three peaks that form a distinct trident shape, creating an awe-inspiring silhouette against the sky.

The peak is not only a visual spectacle but also a favorite among mountaineers and trekkers. The first successful ascent of Trishul Peak was made in 1907, marking it as one of the earliest high-altitude climbs in the Himalayas. Today, it continues to attract adventurers from around the world who are drawn to its challenging trails and breathtaking views.

From Auli, the peak is often visible, especially on clear days, dominating the horizon and adding to the region's stunning beauty. The snow-covered peaks glisten in the sunlight, creating a mesmerizing contrast with the deep blue sky. The surrounding area offers excellent opportunities for skiing, trekking, and other outdoor activities, making Trishul Peak a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Auli. Whether you're an adventure seeker or simply someone who appreciates natural beauty, Trishul Peak is a symbol of the grandeur and majesty of the Himalayas.
