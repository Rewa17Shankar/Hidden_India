Sarbal Lake: A Hidden Gem in Kashmir

Saral Lake is located on border of Neelam and Kaghan Valley in Pakistan at an altitude of 13,600 feet (4,100 m).
The lake is accessible from Sharda by a jeepable track which leads to Gumot National Park and then through hiking trek can be reached to the lake

Nestled in the serene landscape of Kashmir, Sarbal Lake is a pristine, lesser-known destination that captivates with its untouched beauty. Located near the base of the majestic Amarnath peak, this glacial lake remains a hidden gem, away from the throngs of tourists that usually flock to more popular spots.

The journey to Sarbal Lake is an adventure in itself, involving a trek through lush meadows, dense forests, and rocky terrains. The trail offers breathtaking views of snow-capped mountains, making it a paradise for nature lovers and trekking enthusiasts. As you approach the lake, the landscape transforms into a tranquil setting, with the crystal-clear waters reflecting the surrounding peaks like a mirror.

Sarbal Lake is not just a feast for the eyes but also holds spiritual significance for locals. It is considered sacred, and its serene environment adds to the sense of peace and reflection that many visitors experience here.

Whether you're seeking adventure, tranquility, or simply a place to connect with nature, Sarbal Lake offers an unforgettable experience in the heart of Kashmir's unspoiled wilderness..

Tosa Maidan: The Unexplored Meadow of Kashmir

Tosa Maidan, also known as Toshamaidan, is a 65 sq km meadow and hill station in the Khag area of the Budgam district in Jammu and Kashmir, India. It's located in the Pirpanjal range, about 45 km from Srinagar and 10 km from Khag, at an altitude of 10,395 ft. Tosa Maidan is known as the "King of Meadows" and is a popular tourist destination

Tosa Maidan is famous for its historical background and as a pasture. The name also refers to the historic route into the Kashmir Valley from the Poonch Valley. The area is surrounded by dense forests and is a starting point for many trekking trails. Some say it's an ideal place for less crowded treks. One trek, the Tosamaidan and Greater Lakes Trek, takes hikers through picturesque trails to high-altitude lakes like Damam sar, Gadtar-sar, Bodh Gadtasar, Sandooq Sar, and Bante sar.

To get to Tosa Maidan, you can fly into Srinagar airport and then drive 70 km to the base camp. The drive includes a rough, 7 km off-road section from Sithaharan to Tosa Maidan. Some recommend bringing a 4x4 vehicle because the weather can be unpredictable.

Tosa Maidan, a vast meadow nestled in the Budgam district of Kashmir, is a stunning example of the region's untouched natural beauty. Once a restricted area due to its use as an artillery range by the Indian Army, Tosa Maidan has since been opened to the public, revealing its lush green pastures, vibrant wildflowers, and panoramic views of the surrounding mountains. The meadow, often referred to as the "Hidden Jewel of Kashmir," is ideal for trekking, camping, and nature walks. The expansive landscape, dotted with dense forests and crossed by sparkling streams, offers a sense of peace and seclusion rarely found in more crowded tourist spots. The area's rich biodiversity is another attraction, with various bird species and wildlife making it a paradise for nature enthusiasts.

Tosa Maidan is also steeped in local culture and history. The meadow has been a traditional grazing ground for shepherds and is surrounded by villages that offer a glimpse into the simple, pastoral life of Kashmir. For those seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure, Tosa Maidan provides an unforgettable experience, combining breathtaking natural beauty with a deep connection to the region's cultural heritage.

Yus Marg: The Meadow of Jesus

Yusmarg is known for its grassy pastures and deep forests, with the snow-covered mountains forming a stunning backdrop. Around 2 hours (90 km) away from Gulmarg, pristine Yusmarg is one of the less-frequented tourist spots in the Kashmir Valley.

Doodh Ganga, translating to “River of Milk,” is a serene watercourse that meanders through the heart of Yusmarg, adding to the tranquility of the surroundings. The river is fed by melting snow from the surrounding mountains, contributing to its crystal-clear and refreshing waters.

The potent River Doodh Ganga rises from several high-altitude peaks and a distributory of the same flows into the reservoir. Tord 'Yus' is said to be a short form of Youza or Jesus and a marg means a meadow, it is believed that Jesus passed through this valley while travelling into Kashmir.
Yusmarg, often referred to as the "Meadow of Jesus," is a breathtakingly beautiful hill station located in the heart of Kashmir. Situated about 47 kilometers from Srinagar, Yusmarg is a serene escape into nature, far removed from the hustle and bustle of city life. This pristine destination is known for its rolling meadows, dense pine forests, and stunning views of snow-capped mountains. The name Yusmarg is believed to be derived from the legend that Jesus Christ once visited this area, adding a layer of mystique to its already enchanting landscape. The lush meadows stretch out as far as the eye can see, dotted with wildflowers in the spring and blanketed in snow during winter. The Doodh Ganga River flows nearby, adding to the tranquil atmosphere with its gentle, soothing sound.

Yusmarg is a haven for nature lovers, trekkers, and those seeking solitude. Popular activities include trekking to nearby sites like Nilnag Lake, horse riding through the picturesque meadows, and simply soaking in the natural beauty that surrounds you. With its unspoiled charm and serene ambiance, Yusmarg offers a perfect retreat for anyone looking to experience the untouched splendor of Kashmir.
